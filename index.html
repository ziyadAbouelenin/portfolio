About

Systems thinker.
Hardware builder.

I'm a Ph.D. candidate at Rutgers University's Lab for Machines, Manufacturing & Mechatronics, developing real-time control algorithms for robotic manipulators. My work sits at the intersection of mechanical design, electronics, and software — building reliable robots from the ground up.

Before Rutgers I interned at NASA Marshall Space Flight Center modeling vibration isolation for aerospace composites, and worked at Panacea Technologies where a Python tool I built reduced development time by 95%. I hold a B.Sc. in Mechatronics from The German University in Cairo and have conducted research at NYU Tandon and Stevens Institute of Technology.

Outside research I build things that ship — including Rusookh (رسوخ), a Quran memorization tracker PWA I designed and built solo.
